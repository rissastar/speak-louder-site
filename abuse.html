Domestic Violence & Women’s Abuse

“Sometimes the ones we love the most are the ones who hurt us the worst.”

Domestic violence is more than physical—it’s emotional, psychological, verbal, and financial. It traps people in cycles of fear and control, often silently. My story, like so many others, is painful to tell but necessary to share.

My Story

(Names have been changed for privacy.)

My relationship with “Jack” started off like any fairy tale. He was charming, protective, and knew how to make me feel needed. But over time, love turned to control. Then anger. Then abuse—emotional, verbal, and eventually physical. I left. But not for long.

That’s when I met “Stu”, someone I didn’t end up being in a relationship with but still experienced manipulation and emotional harm from. Then came “Todd” who felt like a safe place... until that turned into another cycle of emotional harm.

I went back to “Jack” after all of it. That’s how these patterns work—trauma bonds. Shame. Hope. I fought to love people who didn’t know how to love me safely. I fought to survive.

What Abuse Can Look Like

  • Constant criticism or humiliation
  • Control over money, friends, or movement
  • Jealousy disguised as “love”
  • Gaslighting – making you doubt your reality
  • Physical violence or threats
  • Using children or pets to manipulate

How to Help Yourself or Someone Else

  • Tell someone. You are not alone.
  • Make a safety plan (where to go, who to call)
  • Document evidence (photos, texts, injuries)
  • Reach out to a domestic violence shelter or hotline
  • Support survivors without judgment
  • Believe them. Don’t ask, “Why didn’t you just leave?”
