Cirrhosis Awareness & Advocacy

Cirrhosis is a late-stage liver disease marked by scarring (fibrosis) of the liver. It happens after long-term, continuous damage to the liver and may be caused by many different liver diseases and conditions, such as hepatitis and chronic alcohol use.

What Causes Cirrhosis?

  • Chronic alcohol abuse
  • Hepatitis B and C
  • Nonalcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD)
  • Autoimmune hepatitis
  • Inherited diseases (e.g., Wilson’s disease, Hemochromatosis)

Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • Swelling in the legs, feet, or ankles
  • Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
  • Easy bruising and bleeding
  • Loss of appetite and nausea

My Journey

Living with cirrhosis alongside cystic fibrosis has been overwhelming. There are days where I’ve felt like my body was giving up on me—but I’ve learned to fight for my health and speak up for what I need. My journey has included countless hospital visits, pain, frustration, and hard choices. But it has also been filled with strength, growth, and moments of clarity. No one should feel alone dealing with this disease.

How You Can Help

  • Share educational content to raise awareness
  • Support organizations working on liver research
  • Listen to and believe those living with chronic liver disease
  • Donate to causes fighting for liver transplant funding and care

Important Facts

  • Cirrhosis is the 12th leading cause of death by disease in the U.S.
  • It can be silent and go undiagnosed for years
  • Early detection and treatment are critical
  • Liver transplant is often the last option
