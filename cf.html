üåü What Is Cystic Fibrosis?

Cystic Fibrosis (CF) is a genetic disease that causes thick, sticky mucus to build up in the lungs, digestive tract, and other organs. This mucus clogs airways and traps bacteria, leading to infections, lung damage, and digestive issues.

CF affects about 30,000 people in North America. It‚Äôs a life-long condition requiring daily care, medication, and frequent hospital visits.

üî¨ CF Facts

  • CF is caused by mutations in the CFTR gene.
  • The average life expectancy has increased to over 50 years, thanks to advances in treatment.
  • Daily treatments include airway clearance, enzymes, vitamins, and sometimes IV antibiotics.
  • CF patients are at higher risk for lung infections like Pseudomonas aeruginosa.
  • Trikafta is a breakthrough medication that improves lung function for many CF patients.
