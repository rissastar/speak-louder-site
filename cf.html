Cystic Fibrosis — My Journey

“This is not everything. This is not all of it. This is not even half of it.”

Living with Cystic Fibrosis has been a journey full of challenges and resilience. Diagnosed as a child, I didn’t have a hospital stay until I was 16. But as I grew older, hospital visits became more frequent, sometimes monthly.

In December 2021, at age 23, I started taking Trikafta — a life-changing medication that improved my lung function and quality of life dramatically. Still, the disease demands daily care, constant vigilance, and a fighting spirit.

CF is not just about lungs and medication. It’s about mental health, physical strength, and the community that supports you. I want to share my story to raise awareness and give hope to others facing similar battles.

What Is Cystic Fibrosis?

Cystic Fibrosis (CF) is a genetic disorder that affects the lungs and digestive system. It causes thick, sticky mucus to build up, leading to breathing problems, lung infections, and difficulties absorbing nutrients. CF is progressive and requires lifelong management.

How You Can Help

  • Donate to Cystic Fibrosis Canada or other CF research organizations.
  • Support policies that increase access to medications like Trikafta.
  • Raise awareness about CF through social media, schools, and community events.
  • Listen to and support people living with chronic illnesses.
  • Become an advocate — every voice matters.
