My Story

I never expected addiction to become part of my story, but it did. When I moved to Alberta at 16, I was already dealing with trauma, instability, and the need to escape. I found myself in a world where drugs and alcohol felt like the only way to cope. What started off as a way to numb the pain turned into a daily struggle I couldn’t escape.

Addiction made me feel like I lost myself. I pushed away people who cared, I let go of things I once loved, and I ended up sicker than I had ever been. My body was shutting down, and my mental health was breaking apart. I was so sick I had to return to Nova Scotia, not just to detox, but to survive. While I was fighting for my life, my mom had a baby who ended up being adopted out in Alberta. Everything was falling apart.

Recovery didn’t happen overnight. I had to face the reasons I was using. I had to relearn how to feel and how to live. I had to forgive myself for the things I did when I was hurting. Addiction stole a lot from me—but it didn’t take everything. I’m still here. I’m still healing. And I’m using my story to raise awareness and fight the stigma.

Understanding Addiction

  • Addiction is a chronic illness that affects the brain’s reward system.
  • It can involve substances like drugs and alcohol, but also behaviors like gambling or eating disorders.
  • Trauma, mental illness, and genetics often play a major role in developing addiction.
  • Recovery is possible—and looks different for everyone.

How to Help Someone Struggling

  • Listen without judgment—compassion makes a difference.
  • Learn about addiction so you can understand what they’re going through.
  • Offer support, not ultimatums. Recovery is personal and takes time.
  • Encourage professional help (therapy, treatment programs, medical detox).

Self-Help Strategies

  • Reach out—talking helps even when it feels scary.
  • Write your thoughts out—journaling is healing.
  • Join a support group—being around people who understand is powerful.
  • Celebrate the little victories—every sober day counts.
