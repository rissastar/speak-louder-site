Cystic Fibrosis

Cystic Fibrosis (CF) is a genetic disorder that affects the lungs, digestive system, and other organs. It causes thick, sticky mucus to build up in the body, leading to life-threatening infections, lung damage, and other complications.

CF Quick Facts

  • CF is caused by mutations in the CFTR gene.
  • It affects about 1 in 3,600 children born in Canada.
  • There is no cure, but medications like Trikafta are changing lives.
  • People with CF need daily treatments, chest physiotherapy, and regular medical care.
