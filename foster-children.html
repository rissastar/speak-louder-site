My Story

I’ve lived through the foster care system. It wasn’t a storybook journey—it was messy, painful, and full of unanswered questions. I was taken into care at 11 years old. The first time, it lasted just 22 days before my Nan got custody of me and my brother. But that wasn’t the end of it.

We were taken away from our Nan and placed in another foster home, and after that, with our other grandparents. It wasn’t stability—it was survival. As a teenager, I was angry, hurting, and lost. I ended up running away to Alberta to be with my boyfriend. His family took me in and gave me a place to stay, which is more than I can say for the system that was supposed to protect me.

This is not everything. This is not all of it. This is not even half of it.

Foster Care Facts

  • Over 60,000 children are in foster care in Canada at any given time.
  • Many children are moved multiple times, with little to no warning.
  • Foster youth face higher risks of homelessness, mental health struggles, and addiction.
  • Indigenous children are drastically overrepresented in Canadian foster care.

Foster Care FAQs

Why do children enter foster care?

Children may enter care due to neglect, abuse, or if their caregivers are unable to provide a safe home. Sometimes, it’s because of poverty or systemic issues—not parental failure.

Can foster care be positive?

Yes, some children find loving and supportive homes. But many experience instability, trauma, or emotional neglect within the system.

How can I help?

Become a foster parent, support local youth shelters, donate to organizations that advocate for children's rights, or simply listen to and believe foster youth when they speak.
