Cystic Fibrosis

“Living with Cystic Fibrosis is a daily battle—but every breath I take is a victory.”

What is Cystic Fibrosis?

Cystic Fibrosis (CF) is a genetic disorder that affects the lungs, digestive system, and other organs. It causes thick, sticky mucus to build up, leading to infections and breathing difficulties.

Symptoms & Challenges

  • Chronic coughing and lung infections
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Digestive problems and poor weight gain
  • Fatigue and frequent hospital stays

My Story

I was diagnosed with CF as a child, and my experience has shaped so much of who I am today. There have been ups and downs — from my first hospital stays to starting Trikafta in 2021, which changed my life. But CF is always a part of my daily routine, a constant I live with and fight against.

How You Can Help

  • Support CF research and organizations
  • Advocate for better healthcare access and coverage
  • Raise awareness to break down stigma
  • Be kind and patient with those living with chronic illness

FAQs

Is CF contagious?

No, Cystic Fibrosis is a genetic condition and cannot be spread from person to person.

Can people with CF live normal lives?

With treatment advances like Trikafta, many people with CF live longer, fuller lives than ever before.

How is CF diagnosed?

CF is diagnosed through newborn screening, sweat tests, and genetic testing.
