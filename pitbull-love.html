My Story with Zayah

Zayah isn’t just my dog—she’s my family, my comfort, my reason to smile when everything feels like too much. She's a one-year-old Pitbull with the biggest heart and the gentlest soul. She’s helped me through some of my darkest days, just by being by my side.

When people hear "Pitbull," they often jump to conclusions. Aggressive. Dangerous. Unpredictable. But when people meet Zayah, they see the truth. She's sweet, loyal, cuddly, and full of love. She doesn't fit the stereotype—and the truth is, most Pitbulls don't.

Advocating for Zayah also means advocating for every misunderstood dog out there. Pitbulls don’t need to be feared—they need to be loved and protected from the hate that humans created around them.

Pitbull Myths vs. Facts

  • Myth: Pitbulls are naturally aggressive.
    Fact: No breed is born aggressive—behavior is shaped by environment, training, and treatment.
  • Myth: Pitbulls have locking jaws.
    Fact: This is scientifically false. Pitbulls do not have any special jaw mechanism.
  • Myth: Pitbulls are only good as guard dogs.
    Fact: Pitbulls make amazing family pets, therapy dogs, and cuddle buddies.
  • Myth: You can’t trust Pitbulls around children.
    Fact: Many Pitbulls are incredibly gentle and patient with kids. It all depends on how they’re raised.

How You Can Help

  • Speak up when you hear misinformation.
  • Adopt, don’t shop—many loving Pitbulls wait in shelters for homes.
  • Support breed-neutral legislation and speak out against BSL (Breed Specific Legislation).
  • Educate others with facts and compassion.
