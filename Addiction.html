Addiction Awareness

Addiction is a complex, chronic condition that affects the brain and behavior. It’s more than just a bad habit—it’s a medical condition that deserves understanding, not judgment.

What Is Addiction?

Addiction is when a person compulsively uses substances or engages in behaviors despite harmful consequences. This can include drugs, alcohol, gambling, and even certain relationships or patterns.

Common Types of Addiction

  • Alcohol Use Disorder
  • Substance Use Disorder (drugs, prescriptions)
  • Nicotine Addiction
  • Behavioral Addictions (gambling, shopping, internet)

Signs of Addiction

  • Loss of control over use
  • Increased tolerance
  • Neglecting responsibilities
  • Withdrawal symptoms
  • Continued use despite harm

My Perspective

I’ve seen how addiction can sneak in quietly and wreck lives—my own included. It’s not something I ever imagined I’d face, but life can get hard. Addiction is about more than substances—it’s often about pain, trauma, and trying to cope. If you’re struggling, know this: you’re not broken. You are not alone. Recovery is possible, and worth every ounce of the fight.

How to Help Someone With Addiction

  • Offer non-judgmental support
  • Encourage professional help (therapy, rehab, etc.)
  • Educate yourself about addiction
  • Set healthy boundaries
  • Listen and validate their experience

Helping Yourself

  • Talk to someone you trust
  • Reach out to support groups or hotlines
  • Practice self-care and forgiveness
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for professional help
  • Take one step at a time. Progress, not perfection.

Support Resources

  • Canadian Mental Health Association (CMHA)
  • National Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (US)
  • Kids Help Phone (Canada): 1-800-668-6868
  • AA/NA Meetings in your area
